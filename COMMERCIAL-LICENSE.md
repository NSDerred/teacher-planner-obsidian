the two licences.

## When you need a commercial license

The GPL-3.0 default is the right choice for most users:

- Individuals using the plugin in their personal Obsidian vault
- Teachers, students, and schools using it for educational purposes
- Forks and contributions that stay under GPL-3.0
- Any other context where GPL's copyleft is acceptable to you

You need a **commercial license** if you want to:

- Embed Teacher Planner (or substantial parts of its code) into a commercial
  product without releasing your product's source under GPL-3.0
- Distribute a modified version under a different licence
- Use the code in a context where GPL's source-disclosure requirement is
  not acceptable to you or your organisation

> **Note:** Running the plugin inside your own Obsidian vault — whether
> you're a teacher, a school, or a company employee — is **always** fine
> under GPL-3.0. The GPL's copyleft only kicks in when you *distribute*
> modified versions or derivative works.

## Frequently asked questions

**Q: Is my school's use "commercial"?**
A: No. Schools using the plugin internally are end users, not commercial
redistributors. GPL-3.0 covers it.

**Q: Can my employer use this?**
A: Yes, under GPL-3.0. Anyone running the plugin in Obsidian is an end
user, not a redistributor.

**Q: I want to ship Teacher Planner inside a paid SaaS product.**
A: That requires a commercial license. Contact the author.

**Q: I want to fork it and submit a PR back to the main repo.**
A: GPL-3.0 covers that perfectly. No commercial license needed.

**Q: What does "distribution" mean under GPL?**
A: Giving copies of the software (or a modified version) to people outside
your organisation. Internal use is not distribution.
